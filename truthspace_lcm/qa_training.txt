# Q&A Training Data for Pattern Transfer
# 
# These are general knowledge statements NOT about literature.
# The goal is to see if the structural patterns transfer to literary queries.
#
# Format: Declarative sentences about historical figures.
# Each sentence teaches a pattern that should transfer to literary characters.

# === WHO IS patterns (character introductions) ===

Einstein is a scientist from Germany who developed the theory of relativity.
Einstein thought deeply about physics and changed our understanding of the universe.
Marie Curie is a scientist from Poland who discovered radium.
Curie worked tirelessly in her laboratory often involving Pierre.
Napoleon is a leader from France who conquered much of Europe.
Napoleon commanded armies and fought many battles often involving Wellington.
Cleopatra is a queen from Egypt who ruled during ancient times.
Cleopatra spoke many languages and negotiated with Caesar.
Shakespeare is a writer from England who created many famous plays.
Shakespeare wrote tragedies and comedies often involving love and death.
Lincoln is a president from America who freed the slaves.
Lincoln spoke eloquently and led the nation through civil war.
Mozart is a composer from Austria who created beautiful music.
Mozart composed symphonies and operas often involving emotion.
Galileo is a scientist from Italy who observed the stars.
Galileo looked through his telescope and discovered moons around Jupiter.
