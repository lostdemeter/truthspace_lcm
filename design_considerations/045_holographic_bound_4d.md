### The Lightcone Analogy

In special relativity:
- **3 spatial dimensions** (x, y, z) = where things are
- **1 temporal dimension** (t) = when things happen
- The **lightcone** separates causally connected from disconnected events

In the φ-dial:
- **3 semantic dimensions** (x, y, z) = style, perspective, depth
- **1 modal dimension** (w) = certainty/modality
- The **certainty axis** separates definitive from hedged statements

The w-axis acts like "time" in that:
- **Definitive (w=-1)** = past-like (fixed, certain, known)
- **Neutral (w=0)** = present-like (current, balanced)
- **Hedged (w=+1)** = future-like (uncertain, possible)

## Why 4D is Complete

### Linguistic Completeness

The 4 axes cover the fundamental questions of language generation:

1. **WHAT** (x): Which words to use?
2. **HOW** (y): From what perspective?
3. **HOW MUCH** (z): How detailed?
4. **HOW SURE** (w): How certain?

Any other dimension either:
- Converges to one of these (redundant)
- Is syntactic rather than semantic (different layer)
- Is domain-specific rather than universal

### Mathematical Completeness

Quaternions are the largest associative division algebra:
- **Real numbers** (1D): Scalars
- **Complex numbers** (2D): Rotation in plane
- **Quaternions** (4D): Rotation in 3D space
- **Octonions** (8D): Non-associative (breaks composition)

Going beyond 4D to octonions would break the associative property, meaning dial operations wouldn't compose predictably.

### Conformal Completeness

The 4D conformal group SO(4,2) has 15 generators, but for our purposes:
- **Translations** (4): Move in x, y, z, w
- **Rotations** (6): Mix pairs of axes
- **Scaling** (1): Overall magnitude
- **Special conformal** (4): Inversions

The 4D structure already contains all the conformal transformations we need.
